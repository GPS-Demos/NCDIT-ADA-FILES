Heading (H1)
Introduction to Screen Readers
Paragraph
A screen reader will read EVERYTHING on the page that isn't hidden, which can be both a blessing and a challenge.
Paragraph
Things to keep in mind to make your content more digestible. Screen readers:
List (unordered)
  • Pause for things like periods, semi-colons, and commas.
  • Try to pronounce acronyms but otherwise will spell out the letters.
  • Announce the page title when loading a page or email.
  • Announce headings and the heading level. e.g. "heading level 2."

Paragraph

How to write good alt text
Paragraph
Alt text is the text encountered by a screen reader to describe what an image is trying to convey.
List (unordered)
  • Short and clear, no more than 15 words. Some screen readers or platforms have limits and will truncate the alt text when the limit is reached.
  • Provide quick overview of image's **essential** content
  • Alt Text Decision Tree
  • American Foundation for the Blind's Alt text tips
