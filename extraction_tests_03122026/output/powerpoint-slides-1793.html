Heading (H2)
Introduction to topoBuilder & OnDemand Topos
Paragraph
topoBuilder is a public web application released by the National Geospatial Program that enables users to request customized USGS-style topographic maps, known as OnDemand Topos, that utilize the best available data from The National Map.
List (unordered)
  • Exclusive to topoBuilder is the ability to center maps anywhere* users choose. OnDemand Topo maps do not need to align with the traditional USGS map grids so one map can cover an area that may have previously required up to four maps.
  • Users also have their choice of file format (georeferenced PDF or TIFF).
  • The Edit Layers capability lets users select which layers of The National Map they would like to be included in their custom map product.
  • Users then simply enter a valid email address and a direct link to download their unique map will be delivered free of charge. Maps are often delivered within 24 hours but may take up to 5 business days.
Paragraph
OnDemand Topo contours and shaded relief are generated on the fly from the latest USGS elevation data, including high-quality lidar and IfSAR data acquired through the 3D Elevation Program.
Heading (H2)
OnDemand Topo Map Types
Paragraph
There are currently three OnDemand Topo Map Types available: The 7.5-Minute Topo, the 25K Recreational Topo, and the 100K Topo.
Heading (H3)
7.5-Minute Topo
Paragraph
7.5-Minute Topos closely resemble historical 7.5-minute USGS map products as well as US Topo maps.
List (unordered)
  • This large-scale map type provides a high level of detail over approximately 50 to 70 square miles per map and can be used for recreation and navigation (orienteering and geocaching), city planning, search and rescue, science studies such as geologic and geographic field work, and much more.
  • In order to balance areal extent shown and printed page size at varying locations on the Earth's surface, three scales are found within this map type:
List (unordered)
  • 1:24,000 for the contiguous United States and Hawaii
  • 1:25,000 for Alaska
  • 1:20,000 for Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands, American Samoa, Guam, and the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands
List (unordered)
  • An additional customization is 7.5-Minute Topos: Contour smoothing. Users may choose the level of contour detail best suited for their unique map needs.
Heading (H3)
25K Recreational Topo
Paragraph
25K Recreational Topos are 1:25,000-scale USGS topographic maps highlighting recreational content available from The National Map.
List (unordered)
  • Similar to the 7.5-Minute Topo, this large-scale map provides a high level of detail over approximately 50 to 70 square miles per map.
  • New layout with expanded legend and nationally-consistent scale improve ease of use and provide a more uniform experience from map to map.
  • Symbology and labeling has been highly tailored so users can more easily distinguish recreational features, such as federal land boundaries, trailheads & trails, ranger stations, picnic areas, emergency locations and more, from non-recreational features.
  • Enhanced contour interval algorithm for more precise terrain depiction.
  • Unique to the 25K Recreational Topo is the ability to show only National Trails or only Standard Trails using the Edit Layers capability.
Heading (H3)
100K Topo
Paragraph
100K Topos are 1:100,000-scale maps inspired by the 30-by 60-minute map series published by the USGS through the end of the 20th century.
List (unordered)
  • These medium-scale maps are designed to cover an area of approximately 1,500 to 2,200 square miles and are ideal for any application where information across a large area is needed.
  • While 100K Topos contains less detail than the larger-scale OnDemand Topos, they provide enough information for many traditional and recreational uses as well as some science and planning applications.
  • 100K Topo maps also feature additional land cover categories, such as grassland and crop/pasture, alongside the customary woodland tint.
  • In order to support a consistent snapshot of 1:100,000-scale National Map data, content on 100K Topos may be less current than data appearing on a contemporary larger-scale OnDemand Topo.
