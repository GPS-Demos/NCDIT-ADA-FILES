April 15, 2025
Paragraph
Dear North Carolina 911 community,
Paragraph
As we celebrate National Public Safety Telecommunicators Week, we want to extend a much-deserved thank you to all our state's telecommunicators for the life-saving work you do every day to create a safer community for us all.
Paragraph
Our state is so fortunate to have more than 3,000 dedicated professionals working around the clock every day to answer more than 7 million calls to 911 each year in North Carolina. And we know that this past year was especially challenging as our state faced the aftermath of Hurricane Helene, the most devastating storm in our history, as well as other emergencies that required you all to go above and beyond your already sacrificial efforts on behalf of our state.
Paragraph
Your commitment to hard work, collaboration and serving others has an immeasurable impact on North Carolina. We know how challenging your job is, and we are incredibly grateful for all that you do. We want to recognize your contributions, not just during this week, but every day of the year.
Paragraph
We also hope that you and your teams can have some fun this week as we celebrate and appreciate you!
List (unordered)
  • Once again, we are hosting the NPSTW photo contest. The PSAP with the most creative celebration for the entire week will win "Pizza with Pokey". The second most creative celebration will win "Pastries with Pokey". All submissions of your photos for the week will be reviewed, and the winners will be announced at the N.C. 911 Board meeting on Friday, April 25.
  • We are debuting the "Telecommunicators Got Talent" talent contest this year and are excited to see the photos and videos of telecommunicators showcasing your artistic, athletic, musical or unique talent. The winners in each category will be announced at the N.C. 911 Board meeting on Friday, April 25.
Paragraph
Happy National Public Safety Telecommunicators Week and thank you again for your outstanding service as North Carolina's true first responders!
Paragraph
Sincerely,
Paragraph
Teena Piccione, Chair, N.C. 911 Board
Paragraph
L.V. Pokey Harris, Executive Director, N.C. 911 Board

NC Disaster Symposium
Paragraph
On March 25, Kristen, Pokey, Tom and Greg presented at the NC Disaster Symposium in New Bern, NC about the implementation of NG911 and it's value during Hurricane Helene.
Paragraph
The North Carolina Disaster Symposium focuses on the increase of coordination and collaboration among North Carolina preparedness and response partners by providing best-practices, evidence-based data, discussion of national and state trends with the intentions of increasing and improving our states capacity and capabilities. Various topics are presented in relation to health and medical aspects of different disasters and incidents.

Paragraph
**911 Board Resiliency Workshop Series** **O2X: EAT – SWEAT – THRIVE Workshop**
Paragraph
Workshop Date/Time: Wednesday, May 28, 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM Who should attend: Telecommunicators, Supervisors, Managers, Training Officers Workshop Location: Samarcand Training Academy, 3600 Samarcand Rd., Jackson Springs, NC, 27281
Heading (H3)
Workshop Information:
Paragraph
Throughout the learning experience, participants will engage with theoretical and research-based concepts, examples of practical applications, and hands-on practical exercises to address a diverse range of learning styles. The program combines classroom education and hands-on application, enabling participants to develop a systematic approach to optimizing mental and physical health, increasing job effectiveness, and promoting career longevity and retention.
List (unordered)
  • **EAT** - Fueling and hydration for performance, meal planning, and daily implementation of healthy habits for teams and individuals.
  • **SWEAT** - Pre-habilitation, injury prevention, pain science, recovery for career longevity, conditioning for performance, foundations of movement and lifting, and programming in practice.
  • **THRIVE** - Optimizing mental performance under pressure, breathing, yoga, integrated restoration (i-rest) for tactical athlete performance, stress factors, behavioral health, building resilience in high-risk jobs, the impact of sleep on performance, and how to create healthy sleep habits.
Paragraph
The 911 Board is paying for the course registration, which is free to the PSAP. This course is not an eligible Emergency Telephone System Fund expenditure, so per diem, lodging, and travel are not eligible. Course registration closes Wednesday, May 21. The registrant will receive a separate email to confirm registration. Cancellations will be accepted until May 21. For any cancellation after this date or any no-show, the PSAP is subject to reimbursing the Board for a cancellation fee.
Paragraph
A confirmation email with dormitory lodging reservation for Samarcand TrainingAcademy will be sent to any registered attendee within five business days of registration. More information on Samarcand Training Academy can be found on their website

Heading (H2)
Answering the Call at 30,000 Feet
Paragraph
As a Telecommunicator, you typically have a servant’s heart, and that doesn’t change just because you are on vacation! Ask Tim Roten, a Telecommunicator with the Wilkes County Sheriffs’ Office!
Paragraph
On February 28, 2025, Tim was sitting at the Raleigh-Durham International Airport, awaiting his flight to Las Vegas, Nevada for his well-deserved vacation. While people-watching at the gate, he noticed a female who didn’t look well. She looked pale and was acting rather lethargic. As luck would have it, she boarded his flight. About 45 minutes into the flight, a Delta flight attendant came across the speaker and asked if there was anyone on board with medical experience. Tim looked around, and realized he had to act, even though he was on vacation. Tim spoke up and was ushered to the back of the plane, where he met the female passenger, he had observed in the airport terminal. He began evaluating the 53-year-old female, who was complaining of chest pain (8 out of 10 on the pain scale) and had extremely high blood pressure. I should mention, not only is Tim an EMD, he is also an EMT-B and, prior to his job with Wilkes County, Tim worked as a radiology tech at Atrium Wake Forest Baptist in the Cardiac Cath Lab, so talk about right place at the right time!
Paragraph
Tim was given a medical jump bag and a headset with a direct line to air traffic control, the Delta pilot, and a physician on the ground with Delta airlines. Tim was given orders from the physician to administer chest pain protocol with baby aspirin, nitroglycerin, and oxygen. After three nitroglycerin tablets, the patient’s blood pressure had dropped some, but was still very high. She reported her chest pain had lowered to a 7 out of 10. At that point, the Delta pilot asked Tim his opinion on whether to continue to Las Vegas or divert the flight and land immediately. Tim advised his major concern was that the patient could have a heart attack or a stroke, so he recommended the fight divert to land as soon as possible. The pilot took his advice and immediately asked for permission to land in Tulsa, OK. Upon arrival at the gate, the patient was immediately assessed by paramedics and then transported to the nearest medical facility. Afterward, the flight continued to Las Vegas and Tim had a wonderful time on his vacation!
Paragraph
Tim stated, “Some people might not have spoken up, but I knew I had a duty to act, and I feel my previous medical experience helped me to care for the patient, and my experience as a Telecommunicator helped me to relay information to the pilot, air traffic control and the physician on the ground!”
