6. 2022 Technology Committee Goals – The proposed 2022 Technology Committee Goals were discussed. The goals were voted on:
List (unordered)
  • Goal 1A: Develop CAD data sharing technical standards and schemas to enable CAD data portability and interconnection across the NC NG911 service platform.
  • Goal 1B: Develop CAD data sharing access control, auditing, and reporting framework to ensure data security across the PSAP and public safety communities
  • Goal 2: Develop plan for integration methodologies and standards for extension of calls to non-traditional PSAP environments to ensure the integrity of the ESInet, to ensure integrity, security, and regulatory standards and align with the State 911 Plan and the Statewide Communications Interoperability Plan (SCIP).
  • Goal 3: Address technical and governance structure concerning admin lines to enable the eventual migration to SIP based admin lines for re-establishing contact with a 911 caller.
  • Goal 4: Consider framework for cybersecurity policy based on recommendations from the cyber-security assessment completed in June 2021 and collaboration with other cyber security focused partners.
Paragraph
Motion was made by Greg Hauser and seconded by John Moore. Vote passed unanimously.

7. ESInet Update – Gerry Means provided the ESInet update. He advised that Alexander County and Chatham County migrated the week of November 1. Charlotte PD, Fire, and Medic migration dates have been delayed due to necessary router shipment delays. Greene County migration has been delayed due to building construction. Macon County migration was postponed due to circuit installation delays. He noted there were no changes from the military sites.
Heading (H2)
8. GIS Update – Mr. Means provided the GIS update. He noted that 85 PSAPs are i3 ready. The North Carolina 911 Project Team was presented with the 2021 Geospatial Excellence: Catalyst Award. The award was presented by NSGIC at the annual conference in Dallas, TX in September 2021. Mr. Means advised that two RFAI to i3 conversions (Gaston PD and Rutherford County) have been delayed due to vendor firewall firmware issues.
Heading (H2)
9. Business Intelligence Invitation for Bid – Mr. Means advised the contract for 911 data analytics was awarded to AT&T who is partnered with RapidDeploy for Eclipse Data Analytics. The cost is $926,100 for the first year. The AT&T project manager is Holly Cohen, and a kickoff meeting was held November 3, 2021. A 6-month contract extension has been signed with ECaTS.
