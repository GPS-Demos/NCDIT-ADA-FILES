Understanding the Concurrent Call Path Forward.....
Paragraph
Whether you are speaking about the ESInet or SIP administrative service, you can cross the line...off your list. We now use paths...concurrent call paths that is.
Paragraph
A "concurrent call path" refers to a virtual pathway used to handle multiple phone calls simultaneously within a VoIP system, typically over an internet connection. A concurrent call path is a modern, flexible way to manage calls compared to the older, more limited copper wire system. Essentially, a concurrent call path allows for many calls to happen at once without the limitations of physical copper wires.
Paragraph
So ditch the word "line" from your vocabulary when it comes to 911 service here in North Carolina!
Heading (H2)
Transitional Data Management Services
Paragraph
With the start of the new AT&T Contract Amendment, we have kicked off several of the new features. One of those is TDMS (Transitional Data Management Services). The service allows GIS data to serve as the authoritative source for 9-1-1 address validation. With this service, AT&T will replace the jurisdiction's legacy tabular MSAG with a GIS-derived validation to ensure continuous synchronization of carrier subscriber records submitted for SOI validation against the jurisdiction's GIS data. This approach ensures continuous ongoing synchronization with GIS updates submitted by the jurisdiction through AT&T's Spatial Interface and simultaneous support for NG9-1-1 GIS and legacy address standards.
Paragraph
To sum up the benefit: after the implementation, when a jurisdiction successfully uploads clean data to DataHub without critical error, the process automatically creates the MSAG. This means PSAPs should no longer have to manually update their MSAG.
Paragraph
Brunswick was the first and only PSAP to request this service, and with consistently accurate GIS data, they were selected to pilot the project so a workflow could be created. Brunswick successfully went live on February 24, 2025.

FY2026 CAD-to-CAD Cloud Based Subscription Services
Paragraph
For the FY2026 cycle, 21 PSAPs submitted for new projects or recurring costs associated with CAD-to-CAD cloud based subscription services, totaling in a $724,810.59 commitment by the 911 Board to PSAP interoperability for the receipt and dispatch of 911 calls. Board staff are currently working on approval letters to be sent out soon to the awarded PSAPs. Congratulations to those who submitted!
Heading (H3)
Did you know?
Paragraph
If any PSAPs did not submit for FY2026 for the CAD-to-CAD cloud based subscription, but are interested in moving forward with a CAD-to-CAD cloud based subscription project or are paying recurring costs during FY2026 for the subscription, 50% of the costs would be eligible to come out of emergency telephone system funds, provided the PSAP has an MOU with a partnering PSAP for receipt and dispatch of 911 calls.

NENA: Reset, Refuel, Restore Your Purpose in 911
Paragraph
The 911 Board is pleased to announce another training opportunity for you and your staff: Reset, Refuel, Restore Your Purpose in 911 from NENA. This exciting new course is new to NC.
Paragraph
**Course Description:** Working in 9-1-1 can take a toll on even the most resilient professionals. That is why refueling your energy for the profession is important for both performance and sustainability. In this one-day workshop, you'll collaborate with other 9-1-1 professionals and a skilled facilitator to understand the challenges and acknowledge the accomplishments of your essential public safety work. YOU bring the energy to every shift, but energy gets used and must be replenished. In this workshop, you'll learn refueling techniques, understand how to minimize physical and mental job stress, take command of your attitude, take control of your inner voice, and provide support to other members of your team. Failure to refuel your fire leads to burnout, so take command of your passion for 9-1-1 by understanding how to keep your purpose fueled.
Paragraph
**Course Dates, Location and Time:** The course will be offered twice in two different locations of the state.
