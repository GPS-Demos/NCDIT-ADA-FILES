  1. Chair’s Welcome & Goals for Today’s Meeting – Meeting was called to order at 10:00 AM by Chairman Jeff Shipp
  2. Roll Call – David Newberry called the roll. All attendees were by phone or Teams only.
  3. Executive Director Opening Remarks – Pokey Harris delivered opening remarks. She thanked the Technology Committee and stated the NC ESInet project migrations are nearing the end. She stated the 5-year mark is approaching.
  4. February 2022 Meeting Minutes Approval – Motion to approve minutes made by Bo Ferguson. Seconded by Glen Knox. Roll call vote was conducted and the motion carried unanimously.

  1. ESInet Update – Tom Rogers provided the ESInet update. He stated Greene County was the next scheduled migration on March 23. He stated the RapidDeploy Eclipse project is moving forward and virtual roadshows for the PSAPs were held February 23, 24, and 25.
  2. NMAC Update – Tom Rogers provided the NMAC update. He introduced Greg Dotson who is the new NMAC Manager and started February 28, 2022. Mr. Dotson commended NMAC staff and noted interviews for a Tier 2 technician will be scheduled soon. He reminded PSAPs to contact the NMAC if they are reporting an issue and to not contact the AT&T Resolution Center directly.
  3. SIP Admin Line – Tom Rogers provided the SIP admin line project update. He stated a meeting was held with senior leadership and received approval to move forward with the SIP admin pilot.
  4. AT&T/Intrado Update – Ms. Harris stated at a January meeting, Secretary Weaver requested a detailed report of issues and resolutions. Josh Briggs (AT&T) provided a verbal update. He stated all tickets were reviewed, and selected all related tickets that were not business as usual for the report creation. He stated that AT&T continues with optimization and Intrado Tier 3 has been on site in North Carolina and will remain for at least 90 days past the last day of stabilization of the call handling equipment. Audits and optimization on all hosts will be conducted. JP Flood (Intrado) stated there would be a full audit, daily health checks, and the engineering team is engaged to address any software issues discovered. Ms. Harris reminded PSAPs to contact the NMAC to report issues and not contact AT&T technicians directly. Mr. Briggs stated Viper 1&2 are on software version 5 and Viper 3 is on version 7. He advised a plan is in place to update Viper 1&2 to version 7, which will allow for merging of the Viper hosts. He stated this would allow SIP admin calls to follow users anywhere in the state once merged and the PSAP is on SIP. Mr. Flood further explained there would be one large node system instead of 3 two-node systems. Red Grasso asked if issues are also being measured by duration before moving the priority. Mr. Briggs stated the SLA is used for management of the tickets. Patrick Hoover stated on March 2, 2022, there was a planned node move from San Diego to Washington that started at 11 PM MT. He stated during this move there was an i3 outage that had impacts across the country. Intrado engineering was engaged for troubleshooting and the issue was resolved at 11:41AM CT. Mr. Briggs stated this was wireless only and land lines were not impacted. Chairman Shipp thanked the vendors for their partnership.
  5. Executive Director Closing Remarks – Ms. Harris noted that Board members and staff will be attending 911 Goes to Washington February 26- March 1.
  6. Adjourn – Mr. Ship adjourned the meeting at 10:43 AM.

ESINet Update (Tom Rogers)

Due to project delays, network build, and vendor issues dates for project completion are now expected to extend untilthird quarter 2022. As of June 8, 2022 we expect 124 live PSAPs.

The remaining three (3) PSAPs: Anson, Northampton, and Wilkes are expected by Q3, 2022. A 100% migration attainment should be achieved with the final three. Anson County is at risk for delay due to the selective router not being able to accept SS7 connection from ESInet. Wilkes is awaiting dates from Motorola, and Northhampton has been delayed by fiber construction.
