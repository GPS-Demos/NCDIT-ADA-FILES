Fiscal Note Review of Proposed Wildlife Resources Commission No-Wake Zone Rule

Contact:

Betsy Haywood, No Wake Zone Coordinator 1701 Mail Service Center Raleigh, N.C 27699-1701 (919) 707-0013 betsy.haywood@ncwildlife.org

Impact:

State Government: No Local Government: Yes Private Impact: Minimal Substantial Impact: No

Authority:

G.S. 75A-3: 75A-15

Onslow County applied for rulemaking to establish a no-wake zone within 50 yards of the Boating Access Area (BAA) in Hubert on Queens Creek, to mitigate hazards to boater safety. There is a sharp bend in the narrow channel with diminished sight lines and heavy boat traffic especially during trout fishing season. Moreover, there are multiple docks surrounding the ramp at the BAA with current plans to add an additional dock.

Onslow County will purchase two buoys and anchors and will mark the no-wake zone at an estimated cost of $1100.00. Two no-wake buoys and anchors cost $300.00 per buoy and anchor, for a total of $600.00. In addition, Onslow County will spend no more than $400.00 for the Coastal Area Management Act (CAMA) permit required under G.S. 113A-118 before placing structures in waters of a coastal county. Two technicians will spend two hours each placing and marking the no-wake zone (4 hours x $25.00/hr * = $100.00).

State Impact Analysis: None.

Local Impact Analysis: Onslow County will incur the expense of no more than $1100.00 for the CAMA permit and purchase and placement of the markers for the Queens Creek BAA in Hubert.

Private Impact: The rule has minimal private fiscal impact. The regulated community will not incur any direct financial cost because of this rule change, but their behavior will be restricted in the waters at the BAA and swim area. However, the benefit of that restriction in this part of Queens Creek is increased boater safety.

Substantial Economic Impact: There will be no substantial economic impact to the public.

*total compensation
